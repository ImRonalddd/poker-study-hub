1. Stack Depth Matters

Lesson: stack-depth-matters-2
Core Principle

You always play the effective stack -- the shortest stack involved in the hand.

Deep-Stacked Adjustments (200bb+)

  • Go up in value: Suited aces, suited connectors, small/medium pairs (big implied odds)
  • Go down in value: Offsuit broadways (KJo, QTo), unimproved pocket pairs
  • Reason: Deep stacks mean more money behind. If you make top pair marginal kicker and opponent wants to put in 200bb, you are in trouble.

Shallow-Stacked Adjustments (20-30bb)

  • All pairs and big cards become powerful (one pair is often enough)
  • Going all-in over initial raise is strong when you have ~10x the raise or fewer
  • If a raise puts in >30% of your stack, just go all-in instead (you're pot-committed anyway)

2. Position

Lesson: position-2

Why Position Is Vital

  1. Information: See opponent's action before you decide
  2. Pot control: Can always add one more bet (raise on river with nuts)
  3. Equity realization: IP realizes more equity because you see more streets cheaply
Position Hierarchy

BTN > CO > HJ > LJ > BB > SB

Key Rules

  • From early position: play very tightly (strong hands only)
  • From BTN: play substantially wider (43%+ of hands at 100bb)
  • From blinds: positional disadvantage is permanent (flop/turn/river OOP)
  • BB closing action preflop does NOT justify playing any two cards
  • Positional disadvantage grows as stacks get deeper

3. Preflop Bet Sizing

Lesson: preflop-bet-sizing-2
Universal Formula: Pot-Sized Raise $$\text{Pot-Sized Raise} = 3 \times \text{Last Bet} + \text{Dead Money in Pot}$$

Recommended Sizes (100bb+)

ScenarioIn PositionOut of Position
When folded to3.5bb3.5bb
Over 1 limper5bb5bb
Over N limpers5 + 1bb per extra limper+1bb more
Facing a raise3.3x the raise4.3x the raise
Facing a 3-bet (you raised)~3x the 3-bet~3.5x the 3-bet
Key Sizing Rules
  • As stacks get shallower, all raise sizes decrease
  • If raise puts in >30% of your stack, go all-in instead
  • With ~10x the initial raise or fewer chips, all-in over initial raise is usually best
  • With >10x the initial raise, use a non-all-in re-raise

Exploitative Sizing

  • Against strong players: same size with all hands (don't give away information)
  • Against calling stations: raise bigger with premiums, smaller with speculative hands
  • Against players who fold to big raises: raise big as a bluff over limpers

4. Range Composition

Lesson: preflop-range-composition-2

Three Types of Preflop Ranges

TypeDefinitionWhen Used
LinearBest hands in descending order (AA, KK, QQ, AK, AQ...)Open-raising; 3-betting OOP; vs weak/tight opponents
PolarizedBest hands + bluffs, skipping middle3-betting IP; 4-betting; 5-betting
CondensedMedium-strength hands only (no premiums, no junk)Calling a raise (callers are almost always condensed)

Capped vs Uncapped Ranges

  • Capped: Range missing nut hands (e.g., caller who would have 3-bet all aces)
  • Uncapped: Range contains nut hands
  • Pre-flop caller is usually capped on high-card boards
  • UTG raiser is usually capped on low, connected boards (e.g., 7-6-5)

5. Implied Odds & Reverse Implied Odds

Lesson: implied-odds-and-reverse-implied-odds
Implied Odds Formula $$\text{Implied Odds} = \frac{\text{Remaining Stacks} + \text{Current Pot}}{\text{Amount to Call}}$$

Thresholds for Continuing

Hand TypeRequired Implied OddsNotes
Small pairs (22-66)10:1Make set ~1 in 9 times
Suited connectors & suited aces20:1Need nutted hands to get paid

Key Adjustments

  • In position: Accept slightly worse implied odds (better equity realization)
  • Out of position: Need the full 20:1 for suited connectors
  • You will never fully realize implied odds -- discount accordingly
  • Against maniacs: realize more IO (they'll pay off)
  • Against tight players: realize more IO (they only have premiums to pay you with)
Reverse Implied Odds

Hands that tend to be dominated when they hit (top pair bad kicker).

  • Offsuit broadways (KJo, QTo, ATo): top pair + opponent money in = often behind
  • Suited hands inherently have better IO than offsuit (can make flushes)
  • As stacks get deeper, reverse implied odds hands get worse
  • As stacks get shallower, reverse implied odds hands get better

6. Minimum Defense Frequency (MDF)

Lesson: minimum-defense-frequency-2
Formula $$\text{MDF} = 1 - \frac{\text{Opponent's Bet}}{\text{Opponent's Bet} + \text{Pot}}$$

Key Points

  • MDF tells you the minimum % of hands to continue with to prevent opponent from auto-profiting with bluffs
  • Before the flop: strive to over-defend compared to MDF
  • MDF is divided among all players remaining in the pot
  • MDF does NOT always apply post-flop (especially OOP deep-stacked where you under-realize equity)
  • Typical MDF facing a 3-bet: ~30-40%
Worked Example
  • You raise to 3bb from LJ, HJ 3-bets to 10bb
  • MDF = 1 - 10/(10 + 3 + 0.5 + 1) = 1 - 10/14.5 = 31%
  • Must continue with at least 31% of raising range

7. Raise First In (RFI) Ranges -- 100bb Deep, 6-Handed

Opening Percentages by Position

Critical Rules
  • Never open-limp when folded to you (especially with rake)
  • Do not play obviously bad cards for randomness
  • Widen range if opponents rarely 3-bet; tighten if they 3-bet frequently
  • 6-handed vs 9-handed: add ~2-3 tighter positions before LJ

8. Facing a Preflop Raise (100bb)

Lesson: facing-a-preflop-raise

Facing Early Position Raise

Your PositionStrategy
HJ, CO3-bet or fold only (no calling range with rake vs good players)
BTN3-bet strong hands + bluffs; call with implied odds hands (pairs, suited connectors)
SB3-bet or fold (avoid playing 3-way OOP with worst relative position)
BB3-bet linear range + call wide (closing action, getting good odds)
Common Mistake
  • No offsuit calling in most positions (8-7o, 9-8o, T-9o on BTN = torching money)
  • BB can call wider but still fold junk offsuit hands (K9o, A8o, Q9o)
  • SB should 3-bet to avoid poor relative position (3-way, first to act post-flop)

9. When Your Raise Gets 3-Bet (100bb)

Lesson: when-your-raise-gets-3bet

Default Strategy

  • 4-bet with: Premium hands (AA, KK, QQ, AKs, AKo for value) + blocker bluffs (A5s, K9s)
  • Call with: Hands that flop well (suited connectors, medium pairs, suited aces)
  • Fold: Offsuit broadways (AJo, KQo, KJo) -- reverse implied odds

Position Matters

  • In position: Call more, 4-bet less (realize equity better)
  • Out of position: Fold more, 4-bet bluff more (can't realize equity well by calling)
Implementable 4-Bet Ranges (vs CO 3-bet)
  • 4-bet for value: AA, KK, QQ, JJ, TT, AKs, AKo
  • 4-bet as bluff: AQo (fold to 5-bet), A5s (fold to 5-bet), K9s
  • Call: 99-22, 76s-JTs, AJs, KQs, A4s-A3s
  • Fold: Big offsuit hands, weak kings

10. When You Call and Get 3-Bet (100bb)

Lesson: when-you-call-and-get-3bet

Decision Factors

  1. Are you closing the action? If yes, call more freely
  2. How many players? More players = better for IO hands
  3. 3-bet size? Bigger = fold more; smaller = call more
  4. Risk of back 4-bet? If someone can still 4-bet behind, fold weaker IO hands

Default Approach

  • Your calling range is mostly implied odds hands -- you generally want to see a flop
  • With pairs, suited connectors, suited aces: call (unless 3-bet is very large)
  • With offsuit broadways (KQo, AJo): fold unless getting excellent odds heads-up

11. Facing Limpers (100bb)

Lesson: facing-limpers

Two Types of Limpers

TypeStrategy Against
Tricky (limp with premiums to trap)Limp behind with IO hands; raise offsuit broadways (fold to limp-reraise)
Straightforward (limp = not good enough to raise)Raise similar to RFI range; limp behind with IO hands
Warning: Limp-Reraise

A limp-reraise from a tricky limper almost always means the nuts (AA, KK). Do not call with IO hands if you might get back-raised before seeing the flop.

Core Rule

4-bet or fold. No calling range at 100bb. If you call, initial raiser will 4-bet often enough to destroy your implied odds.

13. Facing a 4-Bet (100bb)

Lesson: facing-a-4bet
Key Insight

Most players 4-bet way too tightly and way too large. Against most opponents: no calling range at 100bb (fold or 5-bet). 5-bet only: AA, KK (maybe QQ, AKs).

Common Mistake
  • AQo facing a 4-bet: fold (even IP in most games)
  • King-X suited: bad -- dominated by AK in 4-bet range
  • Big offsuit hands: fold

14. 200bb Deep Adjustments -- Summary

Universal Principle
  • Implied odds hands UP: Suited aces, suited connectors, pairs
  • Reverse implied odds hands DOWN: Offsuit broadways, unimproved pairs

When Facing a 3-Bet (200bb)

  • IP: Call much wider -- 5-4s, pocket 2s, Q8s, 97s all become playable
  • OOP: 4-bet bluffs come from suited connectors (87s, 76s) more often
  • More linear 3-betting OOP (hands with post-flop playability)
  • King-X suited still bad vs 4-bets (dominated by AK)

Facing a 4-Bet (200bb)

  • Call more IP with IO hands (getting 10-12:1 implied odds)
  • 5-bet very rarely: only AA + a few Axs blocker bluffs
  • Pairs: can usually call in position

Quick-Reference Decision Trees

Everyone Folds to You

Folded to you?
  +-- Good hand (in range for position)? -> RAISE to 3.5bb
  +-- Bad hand? -> FOLD (never open-limp)

Someone Raised

Someone raised -> Check your position
  +-- EP/MP (not BTN): -> 3-bet or fold only (no calling with rake)
  +-- BTN: -> 3-bet strong/bluffs + call IO hands
  +-- SB: -> 3-bet or fold (worst relative position)
  +-- BB: -> 3-bet linear + call wide (closing action)

Your Raise Gets 3-Bet

You raised, got 3-bet
  +-- In position? -> Call wider (pairs, suited connectors, suited aces)
  +-- Out of position? -> 4-bet or fold (less calling)
  +-- Big offsuit hand (AJo, KQo)? -> FOLD (reverse IO)
  +-- Small pair? -> CALL (set-mining)

Facing Limpers

Facing limpers
  +-- First limper tricky? -> Limp behind IO hands; raise big offsuit for value
  +-- First limper straightforward? -> Raise normal range; limp behind IO hands
  +-- Multiple limpers, all straightforward? -> Big raise strong hands; bluff occasionally

Common Errors to Avoid

Top 10 Preflop Mistakes
  1. Open-limping when folded to you (especially with rake)
  2. Calling too wide from the blinds with offsuit junk (K9o, Q8o, 87o)
  3. Calling 3-bets with offsuit broadways (AJo, KQo) -- reverse implied odds
  4. Not 3-betting enough from SB (playing 3-way OOP is a disaster)
  5. Copying small-stakes players' sizing (raising to 8bb because "they'll call anything")
  6. Attacking tricky limpers who limp-reraise with the nuts
  7. Never bluffing over limpers (miss free pots)
  8. Calling cold 4-bets at 100bb (just 5-bet or fold)
  9. 4-betting King-X suited facing a 4-bet (dominated by AK)
  10. Not adjusting for stack depth (playing 200bb strategy at 100bb or vice versa)
