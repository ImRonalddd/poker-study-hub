Recurring Themes

Bet Sizing
Always ask: "What am I trying to get called by, and how much will that hand call?"
Give Room to Blunder
Against tilting/loose opponents, bet small to induce mistakes rather than betting big for "protection."
Read Bet Patterns
Small bets = marginal made hands. Big donk bets = polarized. Abnormal lines = unbalanced.
Pot Odds
When you need to be good <25%, lean toward calling unless opponent is tight and straightforward.
Check-Raise OOP
With your best hands from out of position, check-raise the flop. Not doing so leaves massive value on the table.
Folding Strong Hands
Multiway with a big raise, two pair can be a fold. Multiple opponents = tighter calling range.
The Gift Economy
Small stakes wins are often gifts from bad play. Don't attribute them to skill — and expect fewer gifts as you move up.
Tag Opponents
"Laggy reg," "action fish," "tight passive" — not just "fish." Know exactly what type of bad player they are.
