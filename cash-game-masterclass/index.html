Recommended Study Path

Phase 1 — Build the Foundation

  1. Introduction — Master equity, pot odds, and EV before anything else. These are the building blocks for every decision.
  2. Preflop Play — Lock in your opening ranges. Preflop discipline determines how often you find yourself in profitable postflop spots.

Phase 2 — Street-by-Street Play

  1. Playing the Flop — The largest chapter. Focus on board texture reads and c-betting frameworks first, then layer in check-raise and donk strategies.
  2. Playing the Turn — Extend your flop concepts. Learn when to barrel, when to give up, and how board runouts shift range advantages.
  3. Playing the River — The final decision point. Practice range narrowing, bluff catching math, and optimal bet sizing.

Phase 3 — Refine and Apply

  1. Additional Topics — Adapt your game for shorthanded, heads-up, and straddle formats.
  2. Hand Reviews — Tie everything together with real-hand analysis. Revisit earlier chapters as you identify leaks.

Ongoing Practice

After completing each chapter, work through its crash course for a condensed review, then test yourself with the drills. Revisit the revision guides before each session. The quick reference card is designed to be printed and kept at your desk.
