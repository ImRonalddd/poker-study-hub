PLO Quick Reference

Printable Cheat Sheet — Adam Hendrix — Master PLO Cash Games

Starting Hand Categories

Tier 1 — Premium (Always Play)
  • Aces suited: AA-X-X with at least one suit. Double-suited = always 3-bet.
  • Premium Broadway ds: AKQJ, AKQT, AKJT double-suited.
  • Big rundowns ds: KQJT, QJT9, JT98 double-suited.
  • Kings connected ds: KKT9, KKJ9, KK98 double-suited.
Tier 2 — Strong (Open from Most Positions)
  • Aces disconnected: AA72 with a suit. Rainbow disconnected = flat, not 3-bet.
  • Single-suited Broadway: AKQX, AKJX with one suit (ace-high preferred).
  • Medium rundowns ds: T987, 9876, 8765 double-suited.
  • Connected pairs: QQT9, JJT9, TT98 with suits.
  • Ace-wheel ds: A234, A345, A456 double-suited.
Tier 3 — Playable (Position-Dependent)
  • Medium rundowns single: T987, 9876 single-suited. Open from CO/BTN.
  • Gapped connectors ds: QT87, J986, K675 double-suited.
  • Ace-X single suit: AT93, AQ65 with ace-suited. Position matters.
  • Low pairs ds: 8855, 7744 double-suited. BTN only at 100bb.
  • Kings marginal: KK76 rainbow (25% open LJ), KKQ6 single suit (flat).
Tier 4 — Unplayable (Fold)
  • Rainbow disconnected: A-J-7-2, K-T-5-2, Q-8-3-A rainbow
  • Monotone (4 of one suit): A-K-8-3 all spades
  • Trips (except AAA with suit): TTT-X, 888-X, 555-X
  • Low disconnected pairs: 22-7-K, 33-J-8 single/no suit
  • Danglers 2-5: KT-5-2, QJ-4-2 without double-suit rescue

3-Bet Ranges

3-Bet Value
  • Aces suited (any suit config): ~85-94% of 3-bet range
  • Aces double-suited: always
  • Good kings ds: KKT9, KKJ8 ds
  • AKQJ type ds: always
  • AQQ ds: solver likes as 3-bet
3-Bet Bluffs
  • Middling rundowns ds: T987, 8765, K675 ds
  • Ace-low suited: A4T8, A567
  • Low double-suited: 5678 ds, 6789 ds
  • Principle: unblock raise-folds, play well vs 4-bet
3-Bet Trap Range (Flat Instead) Rainbow disconnected aces, trip aces, 3-suited aces. Also: AKK (flat — terrible vs 4-bet aces), bad kings like KK62 rainbow.

Remember Your 3-bet bluffs (connected rundowns) become your BEST 4-bet calls. Your "value" 3-bets (AQQ, AJJ) become your FOLDS. This is counter-intuitive but critical.

Key Suit Insight Ace-Nine suited > Ace-King suited for 3-betting. With AK suited, opponents can't hold the 2nd nut flush — you get less value. With A9 suited, the K and Q of that suit are out there for opponents to pay you off with.

Essential Heuristics

Preflop
  • If double-suited, almost always open (from reasonable position)
  • If rainbow, need premium connectivity to open
  • 3 of one suit = downgrade one full tier
  • Deuce-through-five danglers = usually fold unless rest of hand is premium
  • Small blind = 3-bet or fold. No flatting in cash games.
  • Open pot-size in cash (3.5bb). Smaller only as specific exploit.
Post-Flop
  • Always ask: "What is my SPR?" before deciding action
  • After flop check-check, pot the turn — they're capped
  • Bet yourself on the river, don't check-raise
  • In position vs a flop bet: prefer calling to raising when draws are out
  • Pair in hand + no other outs = fold to big aggression
  • Ace-high/King-high boards as 3-bettor OOP = always small bet
Exploits
  • BB 3-bets are almost always premium — fold interacting hands
  • When opponents bet small on turn after check-check, consider raising
  • Small river block-bets (25-30% pot) as bluffs are massively under-exploited
  • Table too loose: tighten up. Table too tight: add marginal opens.
  • People don't find enough bluffs — big folds are profitable
Traps to Avoid
  • Overvaluing kings from NLHE — KK is NOT a premium in PLO without side cards
  • 3-betting to "blow people off" — they're calling
  • Calling 4-bets with AQQ or AJJ ds — pretty but terrible vs aces
  • Bottom set multiway — very dangerous in PLO
  • Low flush draws multiway — when you hit, you lose to higher flushes
  • Compounding preflop mistakes into postflop disasters
