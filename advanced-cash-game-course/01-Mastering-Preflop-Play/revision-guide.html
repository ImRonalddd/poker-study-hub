A. Raise First In (RFI)

A1. 8-Max Live RFI (No Rake)

Introduction to Live Cash Games and RFI Strategies
  • No limping from any position except SB (where limp/raise strategy exists)
  • Charts cover 100bb and 200bb; ranges are similar in early position, diverge significantly on BTN/blinds
  • 200bb deep: suited connectors gain value (T9s, 76s, 65s); high cards like K9s lose value
  • 100bb deep: high cards (ATo, K6s) are relatively better
  • Small pairs (2s-5s) are weak from early positions in both stack depths
  • 9-8s and 8-7s are notably bad from early/middle positions -- they make the "bad end" of straights
Key Hands
65s is the power suited connector (smashes A5s when making a wheel). A5s is the aggressive bluffing hand.

SB Strategy (No Rake)

Raise OR limp -- never fold (except ~26-32% worst hands).

  • Raise: premium value hands (AKs-ATs, KQs) + junky suited blockers (Q2s, J4s, 85s, 53s) + offsuit blockers
  • Limp: most pairs, medium suited hands, medium offsuit hands
  • Critical: Do NOT raise all good hands and limp all bad hands -- mix to protect limp range
  • 200bb deep: fold 32% (more than 100bb's 26%) because OOP disadvantage magnified deeper
BB vs SB Limp
Raise ~33-38% with polarized range (best hands + weakest suited stuff); check the rest.

A2. 6-Max Online RFI (With Rake)

Introduction to Online 6-Max and RFI Strategies
  • Raise size: 2.5bb standard
  • No limping at all (rake makes limping -EV; value in winning pot preflop without paying rake)
  • SB is raise-or-fold only (no limp range because of rake)
  • BTN and SB ranges are very similar (~42-43%) because positional advantage offsets one fewer opponent

B. Facing a Raise (vs RFI)

B1. 8-Max Live vs Raise

vs Raise Strategies in Live Cash Games
  • In position (IP): 3-bet polarized -- best hands for value + blocker bluffs + some suited connectors
  • Out of position (OOP): 3-bet more linearly with strong hands; call less
  • Key 3-bet bluff hands: Ax suited (especially A5s), Kx suited on the cusp, 65s (the "power hand")
  • 200bb deep: much more mixed strategies; 3-bet with more suited connectors, fewer offsuit hands
  • 100bb deep: more polarized; Ax/Kx offsuit blockers used as 3-bet bluffs more often

Position-Specific Patterns

  • UTG+1 vs UTG: Fold ~92-93%; only 3-bet AA always; KK, AK mix; call QQ-JJ
  • BTN vs UTG: Still very tight; Queens/Jacks/Tens mostly call; K9s-KJs are key 3-bet bluffs
  • SB vs any raise: 3-bet or fold (no calling because of BB squeeze risk); super nitty
  • BB vs raise: Can call; 3-bet mainly Ax suited, Kx suited, suited connectors as bluffs
Critical Rule (200bb)
AK offsuit does NOT always 3-bet. Often just calls, especially OOP.

B2. 6-Max Online vs Raise

vs Raise Strategies in Online 6-Max
  • HJ/CO vs LJ raise: NO calling range -- 3-bet or fold only (rake penalty for flatting)
  • BTN vs raise: Gets a calling range (best position)
  • SB vs raise: 3-bet or fold; size 10bb (larger than IP 3-bets of 8bb)
  • BB vs raise: 3-bet to 10bb OOP; can call; fold offsuit junk
Key Difference
No cold calling from middle positions (rake makes it unprofitable). 3-bet sizes larger OOP to discourage calls. Against BTN raises, BB defends widest with "magical triangle" of suited connectors for 3-bet bluffs.
Cross-Reference
3-bet bluff hands are consistent across formats -- A5s, 65s, low Ax suited, Kx suited on the cusp. See C1/C2 for how to respond when your 3-bet gets 4-bet.

C. vs 3-Bet Strategies

C1. 8-Max Live vs 3-Bet

vs 3-Bet Strategies in Live Cash Games
  • UTG vs any 3-bet: Super tight. 4-bet only AA, KK, AK (and not always). Fold AQo, KQs, low Ax suited
  • 200bb deep: Call much more (deeper SPR = better implied odds). 4-bet almost never from IP
  • 100bb deep: 4-bet jam with AK + KK as primary shoving hands
  • Calling range: Pairs, suited connectors (especially 65s), AK suited, AQ suited -- hands with post-flop playability
  • Folding range: KJs, KTs, QTs -- likely dominated. All offsuit hands except AKo

IP vs OOP Pattern

  • In position: 4-bet very rarely (almost never 200bb deep); call wide with implied odds hands
  • Out of position: 4-bet more often; use AJo/AQo as blocker bluffs at 100bb; use suited connectors at 200bb
Key Rule
When you raise and get 3-bet -- fold every offsuit hand except AK in most scenarios.

C2. 6-Max Online vs 3-Bet

vs 3-Bet Strategies in Online 6-Max
  • LJ vs HJ/CO/BTN 3-bet: Fold Ax suited (bad ones), Kx suited, QJs. Call pairs, suited connectors, AK suited
  • 100bb: Jam AK + KK; 4-bet to 20bb with polarized range (AJs, KQs for playability, not AQo for blockers)
  • BTN vs SB/BB 3-bet: 4-bet rarely in position; call wide with suited connectors and pairs
  • SB/BB vs 4-bet response: Jam with AK/KK; call low stuff; fold high dominated hands
100bb vs 200bb 4-Bet Bluffs
100bb: Bluffs from Ax suited, Kx suited (offsuit blockers).
200bb: Bluffs from Ax suited, Kx suited, AND suited connectors (playability matters).

D. vs 4-Bet / 5-Bet / All-In

D1. 8-Max Live vs 4-Bet (Non-All-In)

vs 4-Bet Non All-In Strategies in Live Cash Games
  • Against 24bb 4-bet (you put in 10bb 3-bet): must put in 14 to win ~49.5 -- decent odds
  • 100bb: Fold Ax suited (low), Kx suited (all including KQ, KJ); call pairs, suited connectors, AKs, QQ+; jam AK/KK
  • 200bb: Fold KQ/KJ suited, AKo (!); 5-bet only with AA + Ax suited blockers; call everything else decent
Key Rule
AK offsuit folds to 4-bet at 200bb deep against tight ranges.

D2. 6-Max Online vs 4-Bet (Non-All-In)

vs 4-Bet Non All-In Strategies in Online 6-Max
  • From IP (BTN): Jam AK + KK ~half the time; call pairs, suited connectors, AKs, QQ; fold Ax/Kx suited, offsuit stuff
  • From OOP (SB/BB): Jam more (AK, KK, QQ, JJ, TT); fold high dominated hands; call low suited connectors
  • AQo rule: Folds to 4-bet in ALL positions except BTN vs CO

D3. 8-Max vs 4-Bet/5-Bet/6-Bet All-In

vs 4-Bet, 5-Bet, 6-Bet All-In Strategies in Live Cash Games
  • vs 4-bet all-in (100bb): Call QQ+, AK (offsuit sometimes folds vs tight ranges)
  • vs 5-bet all-in: Call all pairs in range + AKs (sometimes fold AKo vs EP)
  • vs 6-bet all-in (200bb only): AA, KK, AKs. Sometimes fold KK vs super-nutted ranges
Heuristic
vs tight ranges = QQ+ and AK. vs wide ranges = TT+ and AK.

D4. 6-Max vs 4-Bet All-In + 5-Bet All-In

vs 4-Bet All-in and 5-Bet All-in in Online 6-Max
  • vs 4-bet all-in: TT+ and AK against wide ranges; QQ+ and AKs against tight ranges
  • vs 5-bet all-in: TT+ and AK. Simple rule: "Tens or better and Ace-King calls"
  • AK offsuit sometimes folds vs EP 5-bet all-in

E. vs 5-Bet Non-All-In (200bb Only)

vs 5-Bet Non All-In Strategies in Live Cash Games
  • Only occurs at 200bb deep (100bb would be all-in by 5-bet)
  • In position: Call everything in your range (getting great odds against click-back 5-bets)
  • Out of position: Jam AA, KK, AK; call QQ, AQs, suited connectors
Key Insight
When opponent click-backs the 5-bet (minimal raise), you're getting great odds to see a flop.

F. Squeezing

F1. 8-Max Squeezing vs 1 Caller

Squeezing vs 1 Caller in Live Cash Games
  • Raise + call + you: 3-bet (squeeze) or fold from middle positions; BTN can sometimes call
  • GTO squeeze ranges tighter than exploitative (solver assumes callers play well post-flop)
  • Exploitative adjustment: Play more suited aces, pairs, low suited connectors than GTO suggests
  • SB: 3-bet or fold only; no cold calling even with raise + call before you
  • BB: Gets calling range; defend with suited aces, pairs, suited connectors/gappers

F2. 8-Max Squeezing vs Multiple Callers

Squeezing vs Multiple Callers in Live Cash Games
  • GTO charts are "too tight" for real-world play (solver assumes perfect post-flop)
  • BTN vs raise + call + call: GTO says no calling range (3-bet or fold); JL recommends calling with implied odds hands
  • Real-world adjustment: Call all pairs, suited aces, low suited connectors
Warning
Offsuit hands are BAD in multiway pots -- this is the most consistent message.

F3. 6-Max Squeezing

Squeezing Strategies in Online 6-Max
  • Only realistic squeeze spots: SB/BB vs raise + BTN call (only seat with a calling range in GTO)
  • SB: 3-bet or fold; TT+ and best suited broadway + 65s, A5s
  • BB: 3-bet linearly (JJ+ value) + call suited aces, pairs, suited gappers

G. Cold 4-Betting

G1. 8-Max Cold 4-Betting

Cold 4-Betting in Live Cash Games
Never Cold Call
When facing raise + 3-bet before you (no money invested): 4-bet or fold, NEVER cold call. Cold calling the 3-bet reveals a "pretty good but not premium" hand -- very exploitable.
  • Standard range: QQ+, AK, AQs, A5s as the primary bluff
  • Against tightest scenarios (LJ raise + HJ 3-bet): QQ+, AK, A5s only
  • Against widest (CO raise + BTN 3-bet): expand to JJ, AJo/ATo suited, K10s+

G2. 6-Max Cold 4-Betting

Cold 4-Betting in Online 6-Max
  • Same principle: 4-bet or fold, never cold call
  • Implementable rule: QQ+, AQs+, AK, A5s from all positions
  • From OOP: prefer AQs over A5s as bluff (better playability)
  • Against wider ranges: add JJ, KQs

H. Facing 3-Bets as the Cold Caller

Facing 3-Bets as the Cold Caller in Live Cash Games
  • You raised or called, someone else 3-bets, initial raiser acts, then back to you
  • As initial raiser (facing 3-bet + cold 4-bet): Fold almost everything (both ranges are nutted)
  • As initial caller: Call with pairs and low suited connectors (live cards); fold high suited stuff (dominated)
Key Insight
Low suited connectors (65s, 76s) CALL because they're live; high suited hands (KQs, KJs) FOLD because they're dominated. When initial raiser calls the 3-bet (cold calling range): defend tighter than when they fold.

I. Blind vs Blind (No Rake)

Playing Blind vs Blind in Live Cash Games
  • In no-rake games: do NOT chop. You can profit if you play better.
  • SB raise-or-limp strategy: Raise ~10-12% (value + trashy bluffs); limp ~62-68%
  • BB vs limp: Raise ~33-38% polarized; check rest
  • BB vs SB raise: 3-bet ~10-12% (JJ+, AQ+, KQ + blocker bluffs); call wide with suited hands
  • Escalation chain: As betting rounds increase (3-bet, 4-bet, 5-bet, 6-bet), ranges converge to JJ+ and AK
  • 200bb deep patterns: More suited connected 3-bet/4-bet bluffs; fewer offsuit blockers; AK offsuit doesn't always 4-bet
The Ultimate Rule
"Jacks or better and AK" -- the ultimate high-action call-off rule for blind vs blind.

J. Playing vs Lots of Action

J1. 8-Max vs Lots of Action

Playing vs Lots of Action in Live Cash Games
  • Raise + 3-bet + 4-bet before you: fold everything except AA, KK, AKs
  • Even QQ folds when facing raise + 3-bet + 4-bet from early positions
  • As the 3-bettor facing a 4-bet with callers behind: Call with low suited stuff; fold high cards; jam AK/KK
  • Protect your range: Slow-play AA when calling range exists (need aces to protect weaker calls)

J2. 6-Max vs Lots of Action

Playing vs Lots of Action in Online 6-Max
  • Same principles: extreme action = extreme tightness
  • BTN facing raise + 3-bet + 4-bet: fold everything except AA, KK, AKs
Warning
"When raise + 3-bet + 4-bet happens before you, you better have the nuts."

Universal Patterns (Cross-Lesson Themes)

The Offsuit Rule

Offsuit hands are bad. In virtually every scenario beyond RFI, offsuit hands (except AKo and sometimes AQo) should fold. This is the single most consistent message across all 20 lessons.

The Power Hands

  • 65s: The #1 suited connector for aggression. Gets in as a bluff in nearly every 3-bet/4-bet spot
  • A5s: The #1 blocker bluff. Blocks continuing ranges; the 5 is live when called
  • AK offsuit (200bb): Counterintuitively weak. Often just calls or folds to 4-bets at deep stacks

The 9-8 Suited Trap

9-8 suited is consistently identified as a BAD hand across all lessons. It makes the bad end of straights (Q-J-T with opponent holding AK) and is dominated by calling/3-betting ranges. Fold it in most preflop scenarios. Prefer 65s, 76s, 54s instead.

The Escalation Rule

Escalation Pattern
As action escalates (raise → 3-bet → 4-bet → 5-bet → all-in), ranges converge to:

100bb: AA, KK, AK jam. QQ-JJ call. Everything else folds.
200bb: AA, KK, AKs re-raise small. Call wider with implied odds hands. AKo often folds.
Ultimate rule: "Jacks or better and Ace-King" for all-in decisions.

Study Order

  1. Start with RFI (A1, A2) -- most common spot, foundation for everything
  2. Then vs Raise (B1, B2) -- second most common decision
  3. Then vs 3-Bet (C1, C2) -- learn when to let go
  4. Then Squeezing (F1-F3) -- multiway preflop dynamics
  5. Then vs 4-Bet+ (D1-D4, E) -- rare but high-stakes decisions
  6. Then Cold 4-Bet (G1, G2) + Cold Caller (H) -- exotic spots
  7. Finally Blind vs Blind (I) + Lots of Action (J1, J2) -- master the edges
