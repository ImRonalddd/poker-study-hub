2. Core Framework: Four Factors Driving Bet Size

Every bet size decision on every street reduces to four questions:

1. Nut Advantage

Definition
Who has more combinations of premium hands?
  • You have nut advantage → Bet LARGER, less frequently (polarized)
  • Neither has nut advantage → Bet SMALLER, more frequently (range bet)
  • Opponent has nut advantage → Check more, bet tiny when you do

2. Range Connectivity

Definition
How well does the board connect with your opponent's continuing range?
  • High connectivity (lots of pairs/draws for opponent) → Bet LARGER (they'll call with decent hands your strong hands crush)
  • Low connectivity (opponent has mostly air) → Bet SMALLER (they fold trash to any size)

3. Stack Depth

  • Shallow (40-100bb): Overpairs are effective nuts → bigger bets, wider value
  • Deep (200bb+): Need closer to actual nuts → more checking, more polarized when betting
  • As stacks deepen, your stack-off threshold requires stronger hands

4. Board Dynamics

  • Straight/flush ALREADY available → Bet SMALLER (current nuts likely stays nuts)
  • Straight/flush DRAW present → Bet LARGER (board will change, build pot now)
  • No draws (dry board) → Overpairs become effective nuts → can go very large

3. Flop Bet Sizing

When to Bet Very Small (~20% pot)

  • Monotone flops (three of same suit): Both players have nut flushes, range advantage without nut advantage
  • Paired flops (e.g., 8-6-6): You lack trips, opponent has them; you have overpairs
  • Uncoordinated low boards (K-6-2): Big range advantage, no nut advantage
  • Boards with straight already there (7-5-4): Opponent has the made hands
Key Insight
As you bet smaller, you can bet MORE OFTEN.

When to Bet Very Large (66%+ pot, often overbets)

  • Uncoordinated low boards where overpairs are nuts (8-3-2, 7-4-2): No straight/flush available
  • High boards with draw potential (A-K-4, A-Q-3): You have overcards + draws
  • Boards heavily favoring your range (A-K-J from LJ): Massive nut advantage
Key Insight
When there's NO straight/flush available but draws exist → big sizes.

The Straight/Flush Shortcut

Board StateBet Size Tendency
Straight or flush already availableBet SMALL
Straight/flush draw but none madeBet LARGE
Completely dry (no draws)Overbet (overpairs = effective nuts)

4. Check-Raise Sizing

Three Rules for Check-Raise Size

  1. Smaller bet faced → Larger raise (relative to bet): If they bet 1bb, raise to 10-15bb
  2. Larger bet faced → Smaller raise (relative to bet): If they pot it, raise to ~2.4x
  3. More dynamic board → Larger raise: Nuts likely to change = bigger sizing

5. Exploitative Flop Adjustments

Bet Large When Opponent...

  1. Is inelastic to bet sizes: They call with pairs/draws regardless → load the pot
  2. Never check-raises: You can bet bigger with value since you won't get punished
  3. Defends BB too wide: More trash in range = bigger nut advantage for you

Bet Very Small When Opponent...

  1. Defends BB too wide: They have more junk, you have range advantage → bet tiny and often
  2. Is inelastic to sizes: They fold trash to any size → make bluffs cheaply (15/80 = 19% breakeven)
  3. Never check-raises: Bet wider for thin value/protection with no fear of raises

Exploitative Check-Raising

  • Opponent auto-c-bets boards good for THEM: Can't exploit much, play near GTO
  • Opponent auto-c-bets boards good for YOU: Punish with check-raises
    • Trash in their range → Small check-raise (they fold air anyway)
    • Marginal hands in their range → Large check-raise (pressure pairs/draws)

6. Turn Bet Sizing

After Flop C-Bet + Call

  • You have nut advantage but NOT range advantage (opponent folded junk, raised strong hands)
  • This leads to polarized betting with big sizes
  • Over-realize equity with position + nut advantage (46% equity → 54% EV)

Key Turn Dynamics

Turn Card TypeBet Size
Flush completesSMALLER (both players have flushes)
High card (K, Q, A)LARGER (your bluffs improved, nut advantage grows)
Current nuts preservedLARGE overbets (150% pot)
Straight completesSMALLER sizes
Connects with opponent's rangeSMALLER sizes

Turn Probes & Delayed C-Bets

  • OOP as preflop raiser after check-check: You still have strong hands (checked flop a lot) → overbets when nuts don't change
  • IP after check-check when nuts don't change: Rarely huge bets (you would have bet strong hands on flop)
  • IP delayed c-bet when checked to twice: Both range + nut advantage → frequent but medium bets

7. River Bet Sizing

In Position (IP)

  • Minimum viable value bet: ~50% pot (thin value starts being +EV at this size)
  • Don't bet bottom pair on the river for thin value if you bet flop/turn
  • If bet and get raised, it's miserable with marginal hands

Out of Position (OOP)

  • Thin value bets: 10-33% pot (can value bet middle pair at tiny sizes)
  • Always protect checking range with some traps/check-raises
  • The smaller you bet, the thinner you can go for value

River Bluff Ratios

When to Bet Very Large on the River

  • Draws all missed (blank river): You have the nut advantage, blast it
  • Board didn't change from turn: Turn nuts = river nuts
  • EV cost of capping at 150%: Lost 77bb/100 in one example; the nuts gains 616bb/100 by jamming
  • All-in for 4x+ pot is correct in many deep-stacked spots
Warning
Capping your river bet size at 150% pot costs enormous EV. When you have the nuts and draws missed, jam all-in regardless of how large it looks relative to the pot.

When to Bet Small on the River (OOP)

  • After check-check on turn: You have range advantage with medium-strength hands
  • Bet thin value (top pair, two pair) for 33% pot
  • Protect small betting range with some nutted hands that can bet-3bet
  • Include a small number of bluffs (lowest equity hands)

8. Multi-Street Sizing

Pot Growth Principle

Pots grow exponentially in no-limit. A slightly bigger flop bet cascades:

  • Small flop bet path: 33% → 50 pot → 200 turn → 100 river = small final pot
  • Large flop bet path: 100% → 65 pot → 500 turn → all-in = much larger final pot
  • Against players who will call with any decent hand, load the pot early
