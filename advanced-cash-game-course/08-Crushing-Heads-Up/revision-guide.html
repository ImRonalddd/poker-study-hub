1. Foundations of HUNL

Why HUNL Is Different

  • No hiding: you play almost every hand, biases are exposed rapidly
  • Wider ranges: button opens ~80%+, BB defends vast majority
  • Huge achievable win rates: if you're the better player, the edge compounds fast
  • Board dynamics change drastically with stack depth -- same board plays completely differently at 100bb vs 400bb

2. C-Betting in HUNL

Warning
Flop C-Betting is much lower than most expect. In HUNL, "frequent" is ~75%. On many textures, you only bet 50-60%. If you c-bet too often, opponent destroys you with check-raises and river bluffs.

Full Situation: A-9-3 Rainbow (Button vs BB, HUNL, 100bb)

Setup: HUNL, 100bb. Button opens ~80% of hands. BB defends ~70%. Flop: A♠9♥3♣ (rainbow). BB checks. Action on button.

Button's range on A-9-3r: Because button opens 80% of hands, their range includes EVERYTHING: A2o through AKs, 22-AA, 72o, J4s, every suited combo. Both players hit this flop roughly equally -- even the BB has A2o, A3s, random 9x hands in their defending range.

Why check frequently (c-bet only ~50-55%): The board is dry and static. No draws are available, so the nuts (A-x) are unlikely to be overtaken. When neither player can make a flush or straight to beat top pair, there is no urgency to build the pot. Additionally, BB's defending range contains sets of 9s and 3s that are ahead of button's frequent Kx/Qx type hands. Button has NO nut advantage -- BB also has A-x hands.

Hands that bet (B33, small): Medium strength Ax hands (A-7, A-6) that are ahead of BB's bluffs and want a small amount of value. Hands like K-T, Q-J (overcards + backdoor draws) as semi-bluffs. Hands like 9-9, A-K that want to build a small pot.

Hands that check: A-A (top set -- slow play is fine, no urgency), A-9 (strong two pair -- no draws to protect against), K-K (strong but check to realize equity without building pot against BB's possible aces).

EV of over-betting: If button bets 100% of range on A-9-3r, BB can check-raise aggressively targeting button's wide range (which contains many 7-8 high hands, J-4 type hands with nothing). BB's sets and aces gain massively from button's overaggression. Button loses approximately 1-2bb per occurrence from betting too wide on this static, dry texture.

Key Heuristics

  • Side card matters: Lower side cards bet more often (unblock opponent's offsuit folds). Higher side cards check more (block their potential folds).
  • Backdoor flush draw = bet more often: It provides future equity and future bluffing opportunities. Hands without backdoors lean toward checking.

3. Turn Play -- The Most Complex Street

Delayed C-Bets (IP after check-check flop)

  • Use equity-rich hands: draws, gutshots, backdoor flush draws
  • On brick turns, be very polar: overbet with nutted hands, check with middle
  • Cap your big size when no new nutted hands arrive on the turn

Turn Probes (OOP after check-check flop)

  • Must have equity: don't probe with no-equity garbage OOP
  • Prioritize: straight draws, flush draws, overcards with gutshots
  • The bigger your probe size, the more equity you need
  • Blocking bets (B33) need less equity; pot-sized probes need real draws

Heuristic for Turn Bluff Selection

  1. Prefer hands without the current board flush draw suit -- those draws called flop and now fold turn
  2. Lower cards unblock middling folds
  3. Having a "pertinent card" for river bluffs adds EV
Key Concept
When you barrel the turn with low-equity hands (IP nut advantage), you must control river bluffing frequency. Just because you have no showdown value doesn't mean you should bluff.

4. River Strategy in HUNL

Simplicity
Jon Jaffe: "Treat yourself like a high school quarterback -- don't give yourself an 800-page playbook." Use 1-2 sizes maximum in most spots.

The "Check-Back Monster" Line

  • Deliberately check back strong hands (top pair+) on the flop
  • On later streets, opponents discount your range strength
  • Caution: don't overdo it. You still bet trips 80%+ of the time on the flop

River Betting After Check-Down Pots

  • Build two ranges: (1) big bet / overbet for polarized value+bluffs, (2) small block bets for thin value
  • Best bluff candidates in big-bet range: highest equity hands (single-suit blockers, backdoor draws that missed)
  • Worst bluff candidates: hands that block opponent's folds

River Bluffing Frequency Control

Warning
If you barreled a lot of low-equity hands on the turn, you MUST give up many on the river. Just because you have no showdown value doesn't mean you should bluff. Brick rivers don't add nutted hands to your range, so don't increase betting frequency.

River Blocking Bets

  • Use blocking bets to target specific hand types (single pairs, ace-highs)
  • Block when you open the door to raises from flush draws and creative bluffs
  • Ask: "Is my opponent capable of finding raises here?" If not, block more freely

5. Exploitative Adjustments

Opponent Profiling Speed

  • Jon Jaffe: "Decent feel after 20 hands, sometimes even quicker"
  • Key first reads: timing tells, c-bet frequency, willingness to use multiple sizes
  • Whether they standardize their timing is a huge tell

The Bet-Check-Bet Litmus Test

Key Read
Some players bet-check-bet as a bluff (strong/studied players). Others simply cannot re-emerge as a bluffer after giving up on the turn.

If they never bluff rivers after checking turn: massively over-fold on their river bets in check-down pots.
If they do bet-check-bet: defend wider because their capped range is less capped.

Over/Under-Bluffing Adjustments

Critical Formula
If opponent under-bluffs by 5%, don't under-call by 5% -- never call a bluff catcher (all bluff catchers become losing calls).
If opponent over-bluffs by 5%, call every single bluff catcher (all become profitable).
The adjustment is always extreme, not proportional.
Full Situation: Extreme Bluff Frequency Adjustment -- HUNL, 100bb, River Spot

Setup: HUNL, 100bb. After 40 hands, you've identified that opponent never fires river bluffs after checking the turn. They bet-check-fold (bet flop, check turn, then either check or fold river). You are BB. Button bets flop, checks turn, bets river with 60bb into a 70bb pot (86% pot).

At GTO (opponent bluffs at correct frequency): You must call a 86% pot bet with roughly 46% of your bluff catchers (the MDF for 86% pot). You hold K♥8♠ (king high -- top bluff catcher category). GTO says call ~46% of the time with this hand strength.

With your read (opponent never bluffs river after turn check): The adjustment is NOT "call K8 slightly less." The adjustment is NEVER call K8 -- nor any bluff catcher. If opponent's betting range here contains only value (sets, two pair, top pairs), you need 46% equity to call -- but you have 0-15% equity against pure value. Every single bluff catcher is a losing call by a wide margin.

Why the response must be extreme: GTO equilibrium is binary for the defender. If bluff frequency is even 1% below optimal, the expected value of calling ANY bluff catcher is negative (you lose the call amount minus your equity vs value-only range). The only correct response is pure fold with all bluff catchers. There is no "call a bit less" -- every bluff catcher has negative EV vs a value-only range.

Conversely, if opponent over-bluffs (always bluffs river after turn check): Their range contains too many bluffs. Even K-4 (very weak bluff catcher) becomes a profitable call. You should call K-4, K-2, any pair -- literally every bluff catcher. The EV of calling with even the weakest bluff catcher is positive because you're scooping pots from opponent's busted draws.

Three-Bet Frequency Reads

  • Opponent three-betting 20%? Their ranges are narrow -- fold more preflop
  • Opponent three-betting 33%? They have K6o in there -- wider ranges on flops
  • "Shiny new toy" tells: when someone just learned a new aggressive line, they overdo it

6. Deep Stack Play (200bb+)

How 200bb+ Changes Everything

  • SPR is enormous: single-pair hands become much harder to stack off with
  • Board dynamics matter even more: don't pile money in with overpairs on dynamic boards
  • Positional advantage magnifies: call more in position because you over-realize equity even more

Jon Jaffe's Deep Stack Framework

  1. Identify opponent's bluffing comfort: do they run big bluffs? Use fringy combos?
  2. If opponent is scared of big pots: inflate pots, jack up frequencies
  3. If opponent is spewy: play value-heavy, flat strong hands, let them hang themselves
  4. "More than anything, understand your opponent's approach before you build yours"

Adjusting Sizing with Depth

  • At 100bb: standard raise sizes work fine
  • At 200bb: increase 3-bet sizing to charge for positional equity
  • At 400bb+: "You just can't fold good suited cards" in position against 3-bets
  • Paired/dry boards: smaller sizes; connected/wet boards: bigger sizes

When to Push Equity vs Check Strong Hands

Key Concept
Push equity when you have added draws (gutshot, backdoor flush). Check back when you have pure top pair with no added equity (e.g., K-Jo on K-high board). This is NOT about hand strength ranking -- it's about which hands benefit most from building the pot.

7. Three-Bet Pot Strategy in HUNL

Three-Betting Frequency

  • HUNL 3-bet from BB: roughly 25%
  • Your preconceived notions from 6-max about board range advantages DO NOT APPLY
  • Three-bet ranges are so wide that boards like K-T-3r aren't clear button advantages anymore

Flop Play in 3-Bet Pots

  • Lots of checking from the 3-bettor (OOP)
  • Boards like A-9-3r: very heavy check frequency; equities are static
  • When you bet, use small sizes (B33) as default
  • Check-raise vs check-call: at 100bb check-raise more; at 200bb+ check-raise less

Managing Mixed Frequencies

  • When you 3-bet T8o and check, you have garbage; finding aggression is hard
  • Solution: commit to reasonable check-raise frequencies on the flop
  • Adapt every node: "You check-raise flop, you get a jack -- you just check [the turn]"

8. Monotone Board Strategy

General Approach

  • Very small bet sizes (B25-B33 maximum)
  • Heavy checking frequency
  • If you don't have a flush draw, it's hard to continue for big sizes
  • Use tiny check-raise sizes (min-raise) because fold equity is high with condensed ranges

The Gap Heuristic

Rule
The bigger the gap between your two cards, the more inclined you are to bet. A-2 suited: wants to bet (big gap, blocks less). A-Q suited: more inclined to check (blocks more of opponent's folding range).

Deep Stack Monotone Boards

  • At 700bb deep on a monotone board: play extremely passive
  • Even top pair is not a hand you want to pile money into at massive SPRs
  • Develop tiny min-raises if opponent bets too frequently

9. Limping Strategy (Button)

  • Limping occasionally keeps opponents guessing your preflop strategy
  • "If I can do something that doesn't give up much EV that complicates my opponent's ability to understand my approach, I'll go for it"
  • Must have strong hands in the limp range (showed A-Qo in a limp pot)
  • Against a limp: raise big, play linear, don't let them peel the deck cheaply

10. Mental Game in HUNL

Recalibrating After Mistakes

  • After a blunder: take a breath, recognize you're now in a different node
  • Don't limit your future decisions based on emotional reaction to past mistakes
  • "No matter what happened here, we're at this part of the game tree and I need to make a good decision"

Dealing with Uncertainty

  • Strong players SHOULD have difficult decisions frequently
  • "If you're not value-owning yourself in spots, you're not betting thinly enough"
  • Finding comfort with insufficient data is a core poker skill
  • Old school miss: "I need to know what I'll do on every street" (builds crutches)
  • New school miss: "I'll do the right thing now and figure out later" (creates too many impossible spots)
